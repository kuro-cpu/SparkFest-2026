Active Threat Protocol

1

RUN (Evacuate)

If there is an escape path, attempt to evacuate immediately. Leave your belongings behind and keep your hands completely visible.

2

HIDE (Lockdown)

If evacuation is impossible, find a secure room. Lock and barricade the door with heavy objects, turn off lights, and silence your smartphone completely.

3

FIGHT (Defend)

As an absolute last resort, and only when your life is in imminent danger, attempt to disrupt or incapacitate the threat with physical aggression.

Fire Emergency Protocol

1

ALERT & REPORT

Immediately activate the nearest fire alarm pull station. Once safe, call emergency services or campus security to report the exact location and size of the fire.

2

EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY

Leave the building using the nearest emergency exit stairs. Never use elevators during a fire. Close all doors behind you to slow down the spread of smoke.

3

STAY LOW & ASSEMBLE

If caught in heavy smoke, drop down and crawl on your hands and knees where the air is cleaner. Once outside, gather at your designated campus assembly area.

Earthquake Protocol

1

DUCK, COVER, & HOLD

Immediately drop to the floor on your hands and knees. Take cover under a sturdy desk or table, and hold on tight until the shaking completely stops.

2

STAY INSIDE & PROTECT

Do not run outside or use doorways while the ground is moving. Stay away from windows, glass partitions, hanging light fixtures, or heavy display cabinets that could tip over.

3

EVACUATE WHEN CLEAR

Once the shaking subsides, check yourself for injuries, grab your essentials, and carefully evacuate via the stairs to an open outdoor assembly zone. Be prepared for aftershocks.

Medical Emergency Protocol

1

ASSESS & CALL

Ensure the scene is safe before approaching. Immediately call emergency medical services or your school health clinic with your exact location and a description of the victim's symptoms.

2

FIRST AID & CARE

If trained, administer basic first aid or CPR if necessary. Do not move the injured individual unless they are in immediate danger from their surroundings (e.g., fire or structural collapse).

3

GUIDE THE RESPONDERS

Stay with the victim and keep them calm. Have a peer wait near the main entrance or driveway to flag down arriving medical responders and direct them to your exact room.

Flood & Typhoon Protocol

1

MONITOR & PREPARE

Stay tuned to weather advisories (PAGASA/local news) and school suspension announcements. Elevate sensitive equipment or documents, and ensure emergency power banks/communication devices are fully charged.

2

SEEK HIGHER GROUND

If water levels begin to rise rapidly inside the campus, calmly move up to the higher floors of your building. Avoid basements, ground-floor parking zones, and open courtyard structures.

3

EVACUATE SAFELY

If evacuation is required, follow authorized paths to designated shelters. Never attempt to wade or swim through floodwaters due to hidden debris, open manholes, or electric shock hazards.

Evacuation Protocol

1

LISTEN FOR SIGNALS

Pay close attention to alarm sirens, public address systems, or instructions given by building floor marshals. Stop what you are doing immediately when an evacuation order is issued.

2

MOVE ORDERLY

Exit the room in a single-file line without rushing, pushing, or running. Leave heavy personal belongings or large bags behind, but ensure you keep your smartphone and school ID on hand.

3

REPORT TO ASSEMBLY ZONE

Follow the directional green evacuation route maps posted in the corridors. Walk directly to your designated outdoor assembly zone and stay with your group for headcount verification.
